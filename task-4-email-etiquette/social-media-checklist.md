# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

##  DO's

1. **Keep your profiles professional.** Use a clear profile photo, a real name, and a bio that reflects your academic and career interests. Employers and internship coordinators check social media.

2. **Use privacy settings wisely.** Review privacy settings on Instagram, Facebook, and Snapchat. Limit personal posts (family photos, location, phone number) to trusted connections only.

3. **Think before you post.** Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor or future employer saw this?" If not, don't post it.

4. **Share knowledge and useful content.** Share articles, academic achievements, project updates, and event participation. This builds a positive and credible online identity.

5. **Engage respectfully with others.** Comment constructively, acknowledge different opinions, and maintain a polite, respectful tone even during disagreements.

6. **Cite sources when sharing information.** If you share a statistic, news story, or quote, mention where it came from to maintain credibility and avoid spreading misinformation.

7. **Report harmful or abusive content.** Use platform reporting tools to flag cyberbullying, fake news, or inappropriate content. Do not engage or retaliate.

---

##  DON'Ts

1. **Don't share personal or financial information.** Never post your phone number, home address, Aadhaar number, UPI PIN, bank details, or OTPs publicly or in DMs to strangers.

2. **Don't post or share unverified news.** Forwarding fake news — even innocently — can damage your reputation and contribute to public harm. Verify information on fact-checking sites like Fact Crescendo or Alt News before sharing.

3. **Don't engage in online arguments or trolling.** Heated online exchanges can escalate quickly and leave a permanent digital footprint that is difficult to erase.

4. **Don't accept friend or follow requests from strangers without vetting.** Fake accounts are common. Check profiles carefully before connecting with unknown individuals.

5. **Don't post content that could embarrass you later.** Party photos, offensive jokes, or controversial political rants may seem harmless now but can affect admission processes, scholarships, and job offers in the future.

6. **Don't ignore cyberbullying directed at you or others.** Take screenshots as evidence, block the user, and report the incident to your institution or at cybercrime.gov.in.
