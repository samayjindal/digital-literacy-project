# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

## What is UPI Payment Fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a form of cybercrime in which attackers deceive victims into transferring money or revealing sensitive payment credentials through manipulation, impersonation, or fake digital interfaces.

## How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. **Initial Contact:** The fraudster contacts the victim via phone call, SMS, or WhatsApp, posing as a bank customer care representative, government official, or online marketplace buyer/seller.

2. **Creating Urgency or Trust:** The fraudster claims there is an emergency — such as a blocked account, a pending KYC update, or a "refund" being processed — and builds a false sense of trust.

3. **Request for Credentials:** The victim is asked to share their UPI PIN, OTP (One Time Password), or to scan a QR code sent by the fraudster.

4. **Money Transfer:** Once the OTP or PIN is shared, or the QR code is scanned (which initiates a debit, not a credit), the fraudster completes the transaction and withdraws the money.

5. **Disappearing Act:** The phone number is deactivated or blocked immediately after the fraud is complete.

## Who is Usually Targeted?

First-time UPI users, elderly individuals unfamiliar with digital payments, students who have recently opened accounts, and small business owners on OLX or Facebook Marketplace are the most common targets.

## Consequences

Victims lose money that is rarely recoverable. They also experience emotional distress, loss of trust in digital payments, and may face secondary risks if their banking credentials are further misused. In India, thousands of such cases are reported monthly.

**Source:** National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (cybercrime.gov.in); Reserve Bank of India (rbi.org.in) – Consumer Awareness Publications, 2023.
