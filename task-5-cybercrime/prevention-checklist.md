# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist for College Students

*Focused on cybercrime awareness for students in India*

---

##  General Online Safety

- [ ] **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account (minimum 12 characters, mix of letters, numbers, and symbols). Use a password manager if needed.

- [ ] **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts — email, social media, banking apps, and UPI platforms.

- [ ] **Never click on suspicious links** received via SMS, WhatsApp, or email — even if they appear to come from known contacts. Phishing links often mimic bank or government URLs.

- [ ] **Verify before you trust.** If someone calls claiming to be from your bank or a government office, hang up and call the official number listed on the bank's website.

- [ ] **Keep your device software and apps updated.** Security patches fix known vulnerabilities that hackers exploit.

- [ ] **Use only official apps.** Download banking and UPI apps only from the Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Avoid APK files from unknown sources.

---

##  Financial / UPI Safety (Mandatory Tips)

- [ ] **Never share your UPI PIN or OTP with anyone** — not even bank officials, family, or friends. Your bank will NEVER ask for your PIN.

- [ ] **Scanning a QR code sends money — it does NOT receive it.** If someone says "scan this QR to receive your refund," it is a scam. Always verify the direction of a transaction.

- [ ] **Check the payee name before confirming any UPI transaction.** Do not rely solely on profile pictures or phone numbers.

- [ ] **Set a daily UPI transaction limit** in your banking app to reduce damage in case of fraud.

---

##  Social & Device Safety

- [ ] **Do not use public Wi-Fi for banking or UPI transactions.** Unsecured networks can be monitored by attackers.

- [ ] **Lock your phone with a PIN or biometric.** Do not leave your device unlocked in public spaces.

---

##  Reporting Cybercrime

If you or someone you know is a victim of cybercrime:

| Channel | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) – File a complaint online |
| **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** – Available 24×7 for financial fraud. Call immediately after fraud to freeze the transaction. |
| **Local Police** | File an FIR at the nearest police station under IT Act provisions |

>  **Tip:** In UPI fraud cases, call **1930 immediately**. The sooner you report, the higher the chance of the money being frozen before it is withdrawn.
